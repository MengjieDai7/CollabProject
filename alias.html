Data Exploration

Vital for understanding the problem

The process of the curation an customization of an automated process for translating and matching alias names to their addresses in the core master address feature class involved four major phases within the scope of the project: data exploration, application of NENA standards, spatial matching and honing, and enhancement of robustness and integrity of the model. Exploration of data included the examination of data types and formats, as well as table and feature relationships, interdependencies, domains, and subtypes. Exploration was also necessary to identify missing values in the core master address point feature class. Working inside Esri’s Arcgis Pro was also essential in reaching a visual understanding of, namely, alias name point feature classes, core master address point feature class, and building footprints.

Creation of Survey

Survey 123 utilizes Microsoft Excel to design the components of the Survey. Through a collaborative and iterative process the fields (questions) of interest to the client were determined and various iterations of the survey were created to align with client feedback.

The survey is designed to captured geospatial location of addresses through a user submitted geopoint and typed out address. The address information entered is then compared to existing addresses. The address information provided by the client is stored on ArcGIS Online and is able to be compared to the new data by calling upon the Rest Endpoint. The survey is then able to determine if this a new address or an address that already exists in the database.

Each time a survey response is reported the staff member in charge of these responses is informed through an automated email. Once the staff member is informed they are able to physically verify the address by going to the location of the geopoint. It is important to verify these submissions to maintain data integrity and quality control.

Organization of Survey Responses

Once survey responses are submitted they are able to be sorted and preprocessed by Feature Manipulation Engine (FME). This process access the survey responses and put them into separate categories of interest (e.g. new address, incorrect address, delete address). This process also cleans up the fields so that survey output fields match the ones in the clients existing database
